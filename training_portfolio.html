Training Specialist Portfolio

A detailed evidence portfolio built during my time as a Training Officer at Emirates Cabin Crew Training, organised against the department's Key Accountabilities and Key Competencies framework, with self-assessment evidence for a Training Specialist progression case.

This is an archival record originally built for internal review. Colleague names have been generalised to role descriptions for this public copy (referees remain named); course-delivery statistics have been aligned with my currently verified figures. Key Accountability 01

Curriculum Design & Development

Responsible for design of learning programmes

Since joining Service Training as Training Officer, I was actively involved in designing and updating Cabin Systems training portfolios, including the Crew Rostering and Roster Swap training. I worked alongside a Training Specialist colleague, coordinating project meetings to establish clear timelines, meeting with stakeholders across the Cabin Crew Service Centre, IT and part-time trainers to gather input, negotiating access to a dummy system for screenshots, and designing lesson plans and activity sheets while ensuring Emirates brand guidelines were met. (Evidence)

I was responsible for updating PowerPoint templates across Service Training to align with corporate brand guidelines — covering Ab initio Crew Portal, CSA Crew Portal, SFS Training Programme, KIS Training, A380 Conversion for seniors and Ab initio Cabin Systems — completed within a three-to-four-month scope, with a focus on font, colour and layout consistency. (Evidence)

Beyond the Cabin Systems portfolio, I worked on Ab initio, Business and First Class workbooks — collaborating with subject matter experts on layout and content, then reworking designs with the Corporate Communications team to fit company guidelines — and enhanced Ab initio course presentations, including a new Emirates Skywards Benefits slide and updated workbook imagery.

Familiar with instructional design frameworks

I created the weekly graduation video, combining images and footage of graduates across the five disciplines of Ab initio training — Grooming, Safety & Emergency Procedures, Group Medical Training, Security and Service — plus the Business and First Class upgrade, SFS and Purser promotional programmes, along with handouts and a lesson plan so other team members could run the ceremony. (Evidence)

In my earlier role as lead Corporate Communications designer at Lamprell, I designed corporate identities, brand guidelines and a full range of collateral — signage, brochures, direct mail, newspaper ads, postcards, newsletters, flyers, invitations and business cards — building the same structured, standards-led approach I later brought to Emirates training material.

Demonstrates an adult learning approach (Brain Friendly Learning 70/30)

I completed a Brain Friendly Learning (BFL) 70/30 workshop and applied its principles to my classroom facilitation, and put forward BFL-based recommendations for the Ab initio Crew Portal session — additional hands-on learning, classroom set-up and training aids — which were implemented.

Key contact for stakeholder groups in design

As a Visual Media & Communications subject matter expert, I worked on a senior-leadership presentation, produced farewell videos for departing colleagues, created training video clips used in the SFS Training Programme, built a manpower-planning statistics presentation for senior leadership, and produced "Amal's Journey" and a First Class service training video focused on proactive, above-and-beyond service. (Evidence)

One of my ongoing responsibilities was designing Flash News, a monthly in-house newsletter for the Service Training team covering updates from across the four Service Training teams. I managed the process end-to-end — collating information against deadlines, designing the artwork to a tile-based, high-resolution standard, incorporating feedback and publishing on sign-off. (Evidence)

Responsible for consulting key stakeholders in defining curriculum

I initiated design changes to the Ab initio and CSA Crew Portal sessions based on classroom observation and learner feedback — identifying password-change errors, improving the logical flow of training, and recommending a simpler password-change process. I reviewed and updated lesson plans to reflect changes such as the new leave-bidding system, the new KIS application, the crew portal's migration to a new address, and updated login and email guidance, then redesigned the equivalent CSA Crew Portal session.

I enhanced the mood lighting session, linking the A380 and B777 control demonstrations to make it easier to use across SFS, Purser Training and Return to Work programmes — coordinating with the relevant Training Specialist colleagues on each. (Evidence)

Training needs analysis (TNA) for curriculum

I conducted training-needs research on behalf of the Visual Media & Communications team — researching camera equipment for potential 360° training demos, exploring e-book formats for workbooks and teaching plans, and resolving a cross-platform compatibility issue for the Business Class Crew Portal overview by testing across Windows, OSX and iOS and multiple browsers.

Key Accountability 06

Business Process Improvement

Identifies improvements for a course/programme

I reviewed how Service Training folders were stored on the shared G-drive, identified inefficient storage practice, and led a kick-off meeting with the Products & Services and Visual Media & Communications teams to standardise file-saving conventions and the use of linked rather than embedded video, significantly reducing file sizes. I followed this up by building an Audio-Video-Image library in the part-time trainers' folder to remove duplication. (Evidence)

I identified that trainer and course evaluations were entirely paper-based, creating cost and efficiency overhead. I built and tested an electronic version of the evaluation forms on Google Drive and OneDrive, including QR codes for quick access on different devices, presented the idea to Training Manager Elizabeth Dales for approval, and worked with the Performance Management Team on finalising the content and questions. (Evidence)

Responsible for defining changes and driving them through

I was assigned the Emirates Cuisine booklet conversion — turning a static booklet into an interactive PDF — researching suitable techniques, completing the conversion within five working days, and presenting it to Training Manager Elizabeth Dales for approval before it went live on the crew portal, which was more cost-effective and environmentally friendly than the previous print process. I received a message of appreciation from Training Manager Elizabeth Dales for the turnaround. (Evidence)

Partners with stakeholder training departments to maximise training efficiency and effectiveness

I worked on the Seat Demo project with Training Specialist colleagues from the Service Planning and Operations Team, moving the seat and suite information from paper-based to an electronic interactive format — attending planning meetings to align on vision and content needs, identifying new photography requirements, researching smart-wall technology for the room, and costing tablets and hardware with Procurement. (Evidence)

Self Assessment

Performance Matters & Self Assessment

Development area

Based on feedback from Training Manager Elizabeth Dales and a Training Specialist colleague within Visual Media & Communications, I identified leadership as a development priority for progressing toward Training Specialist. Through my management of Cabin Systems team members and involvement in project teams, I had regular opportunity to build and demonstrate that leadership capability in practice.

Development activities

Development activities included: completing the Foundation of Personal Leadership and Supervisory Leadership Essentials modules; shadowing a Training Specialist colleague during training-and-development sessions with part-time trainers to observe feedback delivery in practice; and shadowing another colleague to learn and take on CCARS reporting duties, including editing CCARS entries completed by part-time trainers.

Outcome

The outcome was a stronger, more structured feedback practice — using the BOOST model to make feedback specific and evidence-based, completing observation checklists across the Cabin Systems trainer group with guidance on next steps, and eventually being signed off to independently edit CCARS entries and validate the accuracy of feedback given to learners.
